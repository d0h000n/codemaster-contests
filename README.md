## About
 
**송도고 코드마스터** is a programming contest series for middle and high school students. Two editions have been held so far: **Codemaster 2023** (1st edition) and **Codemaster 2024** (2nd edition). Each problem in this archive is provided as a [Polygon](https://polygon.codeforces.com/) package, which includes statements, test data, validators, checkers, and reference solutions — ready to be imported into Polygon-compatible online judges (Codeforces Gym, QOJ, Universal Cup, DOMjudge, etc.).
 
If you need the **full contest data** (complete problem set, test data, editorial, etc.) for archival, mirroring, or educational use, please contact me directly.
 
## Difficulty Levels
 
- 🟢 **Warm-up** — Introductory problems for beginners
- 🔵 **Easy** — Straightforward problems requiring a single key idea
- 🟡 **Medium** — Problems requiring solid algorithmic background
- 🔴 **Hard** — Challenging problems requiring deep thought and synthesis
## Codemaster 2023 (1st edition)
 
The inaugural edition of Codemaster, organized and directed by me during my senior year of high school. The contest targeted middle and high school students in the **Incheon region**, with regional awards open to Incheon students. Students from outside the region were welcome to participate and were eligible for special awards.

## People & Stories

## Usage Policy
 
The problems in this archive are made publicly available for educational use, practice, and reuse on online judges, **subject to the following conditions**:
 
1. **Notify the organizer before reuse.** If you intend to host any of these problems on an online judge or use them in a contest, please reach out to me beforehand. This is so I can keep the archive up to date, coordinate with other organizers, and contact the relevant problem authors when needed.
2. **Credit all listed authors and the original contest.** Any reuse must clearly credit every listed author of the problem, and cite the original Codemaster edition (e.g., "Codemaster 2023 problem A").
3. **No modifications.** The problems must be used as-is. Translation into other languages is permitted, but altering the problem statement, constraints, test data, or intended solution is not allowed.
For any inquiries — including requests for full contest data — please open an issue in this repository or contact me directly.
